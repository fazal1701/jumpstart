### 3. The Jumpstart Model (Step-by-Step)

#### Step 1: Learn ONLY What a Job Actually Needs
Instead of generic "Learn Python" or "Learn Product Management", Jumpstart teaches:
*   **Exact job descriptions**
*   **Exact tools**
*   **Exact workflows**

*Example:* "Junior Data Analyst â€“ Healthcare SaaS"
*   SQL queries actually used by the company
*   Real dashboard requirements
*   Real messy data
*   Real stakeholder constraints

#### Step 2: Operate as an Independent Contractor
Users are not "students"; they are **Contractors**.
*   Short-term, scoped work
*   Paid or unpaid pilots
*   Clear deliverables

| Student Mindset | Contractor Mindset (Jumpstart) |
| :--- | :--- |
| "Am I doing this right?" | "Does this produce results?" |
| Fear of mistakes | Ownership of outcome |
| Waiting for instructions | Problem-solving |

#### Step 3: Produce Tangible, Measurable Impact
Every Jumpstart challenge ends with proof. If it doesn't save money, make money, improve a metric, or ship something usable, it doesn't count.
*   **Examples:** Increased conversion rate, Reduced cloud cost, Dashboard adopted by stakeholders.

#### Step 4: Cross-Niche Validation
Instead of "3 years experience", Jumpstart shows adaptability.
*   **Profile Example:** "Retail demand forecasting" AND "Healthcare data cleanup"
*   **Proves:** Adaptability, Systems thinking, Transferable skill execution.

#### Step 5: Replace the Resume with a Skill Ledger
Instead of a static PDF, the **Execution Ledger** offers:
*   Version-controlled work
*   Metrics attached
*   Employer feedback

Employers don't ask "Where did you study?". They ask **"What did you actually do?"**.
